From demo to production: what changes when AI touches real operations.

A demo proves that something can work once. A production AI workflow has to keep working when people rely on it.

The moment an AI system touches real operations, model quality is only one part of the problem. The system needs permissions, logging, evaluation, observability, fallback paths, documentation, and ownership.

Production also changes the role of human review. In a demo, a human can manually inspect every output. In a real workflow, you need to decide where review is required, where automation is acceptable, and how exceptions move through the process.

Data boundaries become more important as usage grows. Prompts, uploaded files, generated outputs, embeddings, and logs can all contain sensitive company knowledge. The architecture has to make those boundaries explicit.

The work after the demo is not glamorous, but it is what makes AI useful. A reliable AI system is a business workflow with an AI component, not a model call with a user interface around it.
